Customer Profiling and Segmentation

In this project, I utilized customer analytics skills to perform segmentation and profiling on a consumer credit card portfolio dataset. The dataset contained detailed demographic and behavioral information, including age, gender, marital status, income category, card type, balance, credit limit, spending habits, and a Naive Bayes classifier attrition flag for predicting potential customer attrition. The goal was to enhance business value propositions by identifying customer groups with similar characteristics, enabling more effective communication and engagement strategies. Using SAS Miner, I employed techniques such as variable transformation, filtering, clustering, and segmentation. The analysis revealed a predominance of female customers across segments and identified Segment 5 as the most prominent, constituting 28% of the total customer base. This project demonstrated the effectiveness of data-driven insights in predicting customer behavior and optimizing engagement strategies.

Existing Customers

We used the filter node to focus on existing customers and found that segments 6, 2, and 4 were significant. Key variables identified were gender and age for segment 6, marital status and gender for segment 2, and age and gender for segment 4. Females were the predominant gender across segments, with married individuals being more common in segment 2. Segment 5 showed that female gender was less influential. Overall, the analysis highlighted females as a dominant demographic among existing customers and emphasized the significance of marital status within segment 2.

Attrited Customers

In the analysis of attrited customers, segment 5 had the highest count with 278 instances, all female, while segment 2 had the lowest count with 37 instances and unknown marital status. For both segments 5 and 1, key variables were gender and age. Existing customers had a higher count compared to attrited customers, and demographic variables showed higher significance among attrited customers.
