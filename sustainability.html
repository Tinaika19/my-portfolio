Sustainability in Higher Education

Australian universities are committed to sustainability, reflecting their dedication to environmental and societal well-being. Climate change presents a significant challenge, necessitating increased awareness of critical issues. La Trobe University's Future Ready Strategy incorporates sustainable practices such as recycling, composting, and energy efficiency to reduce its ecological footprint. The university aims for carbon neutrality by 2029, with regional campuses targeting 2022 through the Net Zero program, which emphasizes energy efficiency and community benefits. Through the La Trobe Energy Analytics Platform (LEAP), the university collaborates to lower energy consumption. Therefore, we took a look at two types of datasets, primary and secondary

Primary Dataset: It consists of collaboration among students, faculty, staff, and partners that may fall short in generating new solutions to sustainability issues. Supplier engagement and commitment to sustainable practices can also vary, complicating efforts to drive change.

Secondary Dataset: It consists of public opinion on climate change on social media platforms like Twitter, which can be challenging due to the rapid changes driven by external events or viral content. The vast volume of climate change discussions can lead to information overload, making it hard to identify relevant strategies.
